Guava

Soil Type and Climate: Factors such as soil drainage and water retention capacity influence irrigation requirements based on the climate changes.

Rainfall Patterns: During monsoon months, irrigation may not be necessary unless extended periods of drought occur, affecting soil moisture levels.

Age of Trees: Young trees with shallow root systems require for every 2-3 days during dry season. As trees mature, irrigation frequency can be adjusted.

Irrigation Interval: Trees aged 2-5 years may need irrigation every 4-5 days, while older trees (5-8 years) can be irrigated at intervals of 10-15 days during dry spells.

Full Bearing Stage: Guava trees in full bearing stage benefit from strategic irrigation practices, typically involving 2-3 irrigations after fruit set.

Custard Apple

Soil Characteristics: The type of soil, its texture, and drainage properties dictate the irrigation needs, Sandy soils may require more frequent watering compared to others.

Climate and Rainfall Patterns: Prevailing climatic conditions, including temperature and humidity, along with rainfall patterns, influence the irrigation schedule.

Tree Age and Root Development: Young custard apple trees with developing root systems may need more frequent irrigation to support growth and establishment.

Fruiting Stage: During the fruiting stage, custard apple trees may require additional irrigation to support fruit development and prevent stress-induced fruit drop.

Irrigation Efficiency: Utilizing irrigation methods such as drip irrigation or basin irrigation helps optimize water use and minimize wastage.

Sapota

Soil Composition and Drainage: Understanding soil composition helps determine water retention capabilities and irrigation frequency.

Climate Conditions and Rainfall: Regional climate patterns, including temperature, humidity, and rainfall, impact the irrigation needs of sapote trees.

Tree Age and Root Development: Young sapote trees require regular irrigation to support root development and establishment. As trees mature, adjust in irrigation frequency and volume.

Fruiting Stage: During fruiting stages, sapote trees may require additional irrigation to sustain fruit growth and prevent water stress-induced fruit drop.

Irrigation Efficiency: Employing efficient irrigation methods such as drip irrigation or mulching helps optimize water usage and minimize runoff.

Banana

Soil Composition and Drainage: Well-draining soils promote healthy root development, affecting the frequency and volume of irrigation.

Climate Conditions and Rainfall: Regional climate conditions, including temperature, humidity, and rainfall patterns, determine the irrigation needs of banana plants.

Stage of Growth: Young plants and those establishing root systems require more frequent watering compared to mature plants.

Fruiting Cycle: Banana plants demand increased water availability during flowering and fruiting stages to support the development of healthy bunches.

Irrigation Efficiency: Proper water management practices, including mulching, reduce evaporation and maintain soil moisture for growth.

Papaya

Soil Composition and Drainage: Well-draining soils are essential to prevent waterlogging and root rot, while sandy soils may require more.

Climate Conditions and Rainfall: Regional climate conditions, including temperature, humidity, and rainfall distribution, impact the irrigation requirements of papaya plants.

Stage of Growth: Young plants and those establishing root systems require more frequent watering compared to mature plants.

Fruiting Cycle: Papaya plants demand increased water availability during flowering and fruiting stages to support the development of healthy fruits.

Irrigation Efficiency: Mulching around papaya plants conserves soil moisture and reduces evaporation, ensuring efficient water utilization.

Grape

Soil Composition and Drainage: Well-draining soils are preferred to prevent waterlogging, which can lead to root diseases.

Climate Conditions and Rainfall: Evapotranspiration rates indicate the amount of water lost from soil and plant surfaces, guiding irrigation scheduling.

Stage of Growth: Young vines and those establishing root systems require more frequent watering, while mature vines may need less water.

Fruiting Cycle: Water stress during these periods can lead to poor berry development, reduced sugar accumulation, and uneven ripening.

Irrigation Efficiency: Employing irrigation techniques such as drip irrigation or micro-sprinklers ensures minimizing wastage.
