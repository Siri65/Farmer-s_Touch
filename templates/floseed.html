Know About the Seedling Precautions

We provide all the required measures to take care of different flowers...

Chrysanthemums

Environmental conditions: They prefer daytime temperatures between 15°C to 21°C and nighttime slightly cooler.

Germination conditions: The seeds needs to be planted in well-draining soil and maintain consistent moisture levels.

Sunlight: Choose a location for your seedling trays or pots that receives at least 6 hours of direct sunlight each day.

Avoid overwatering: Keep the soil around seedling chrysanthemums consistently moist Water deeply and thoroughly,penetrate the root.

Others: Apply a layer of organic mulch around seeds to help retain soil mositure, suppress weeds.

Hibiscus

Environmental conditions: Protect seedling hibiscus from temperatures below 50°F (10°C), as they are sensitive to cold.

Germination conditions: Hibiscus seeds typically germinate within 10 to 21 days under optimal conditions.

Sunlight: If growing indoors, place the seedlings near a south-facing window or provide proper sunlight.

Avoid overwatering: Overwatering can lead to root rot and other fungal diseases and aphids.

Others: Sow hibiscus seeds at a depth of about 1/4 inch (6 mm) in the growing medium, cover them with vermiculite.

Lily

Environmental conditions: The ideal temperature range between 60°F to 75°Fduring the day and slightly cooler at night.

Germination conditions:Lily seeds can take several weeks to several months to germinate based on freshness, and environmental conditions.

Sunlight: Best in warm temperatures between 24-29°C. Provide a warm, sunny location.

Avoid overwatering: Water seedlings to settle the soil and ensure good deeply and infrequently, rather than shallowly and frequently.

Others: Prepare the planting area by loosening the soil to a depth of 12 to 18 inches (30 to 45 cm).

Rose

Environmental conditions: Avoid exposing seedlings to extreme temperature fluctuations, as this can stress the plants.

Germination conditions:Rose seeds can take from a few weeks to several months to germinate based on seed variety, freshness.

Sunlight: Best in warm temperatures between 24-29°C. Provide a warm, sunny location.

Avoid overwatering: Overwatering can lead to root rot, fungal diseases and aphids,mites,scales.

Others: Prune away any dead, damaged, or diseased branches to encourage growth and shape.

Jasmine

Environmental conditions: Avoid exposing seedlings to temperatures below 50°F as they are sensitive to cold.

Germination conditions:Jasmine seeds typically germinate within 2 to 4 weeks under optimal conditions.

Sunlight: Requires bright, indirect sunlight. If growing indoors, provide supplemental lighting with fluorescent or LED grow lights.

Avoid overwatering: Overwatering can lead to root rot, fungal diseases and whiteflies,spider.

Others: Install trellises, stakes, or other supports as needed to prevent the plants from sprawling.

MariGold

Environmental conditions: Sensitive to temperature, humidity, and sunlight may lead to wilting or yellowing leaves.

Germination conditions:Marigold seeds typically germinate within 5 to 7 days under optimal conditions.

Sunlight: They should receive at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight. Provide a warm, sunny location.

Avoid overwatering: Water seedlings carefully to keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged.

Others: Good air circulation is important for preventing fungal diseases avoid overcrowding.
