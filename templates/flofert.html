Know About the Fertilizers & Prefertilization Process

We provide all the required methodologies to do before and after Fertilization for different flowers...

Chrysanthemums

Complete Balanced Fertilizers: contain a balanced ratio of nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K), formulations like 10-10-10 or 20-20-20 along with other micronutrients.

Slow-Release Fertilizers: It release nutrients gradually over an extended period, providing a steady supply of nutrients to the growth of plant.

Organic Fertilizers: Organic fertilizers, such as compost, well-rotted manure, fish emulsion, or blood meal, provide nutrients to the soil in a slow-release form.

Soil Testing: Before fertilizing, it's a good idea to test your soil to determine its pH and nutrient levels.

Application: When applying fertilizer, spread it evenly around the drip line of the tree,Avoid applying fertilizer directly against the stem, as it can cause root burn.

Hibiscus

Liquid Fertilizers: Dissolve in water and applied directly to the soil. They provide a readily absorbed by the roots or leaves.

Specialized Fertilizers: Some fertilizers are specifically formulated for flowering plants like hibiscus, with higher phosphorus content to promote blooming.

Slow-Release Fertilizers: These fertilizers release nutrients gradually over time, providing a steady supply of nutrition to the plant.

Soil Testing: Conduct a soil test, this will help you tailor your fertilizer application to the specific needs of your hibiscus plants.

Application:Apply fertilizers around the root, avoid contact with stems or foliage. For liquid fertilizers, water the plant immediately after application.

Lily

All-Purpose Granular Fertilizers: Granular fertilizers with balanced N-P-K (nitrogen-phosphorus-potassium) ratios, such as 10-10-10 or 5-10-10, are suitable for lilies.

Liquid Fertilizers: Liquid fertilizers are dissolved in water and can be applied directly to the soil or foliage. They are quickly absorbed by the plant, providing rapid nutrition.

Specialized Fertilizers for Bulbs: Some fertilizers are formulated specifically for bulb plants like lilies, with higher phosphorus content to promote flowering.

Soil Testing: Conduct a soil test to determine nutrient deficiencies or imbalances. This will help you tailor your fertilizer application to the specific needs of your lilies.

Application: Apply fertilizers evenly around the root zone of the plant, avoiding direct contact with stems or foliage.

Rose

Complete Balanced Fertilizers: Contain a balanced ratio of nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K), formulations ratio, such as 10-10-10 or 20-20-20 with other micronutrients.

Organic Fertilizers: Organic options like compost, manure, or fish emulsion provide nutrients in a natural and slow-release manner.

Liquid Fertilizers: Liquid fertilizers are dissolved in water and can be applied directly to the soil or foliage quickly absorbed by the plant, providing rapid nutrition.

Soil Testing: Before fertilizing, it's a good idea to test your soil to determine its pH and nutrient levels.

Application: Fertilizers should be applied at the appropriate times during the plant's growth cycle to support fruit production.

Jasmine

Potassium-Rich Fertilizers: Fertilizers with a high potassium content, such as potassium sulfate or potassium nitrate, can be applied to promote fruit development.

Balanced NPK Fertilizers: Contain a balanced ratio of nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K), formulations ratio with other micronutrients.

Organic Fertilizers: Organic fertilizers, such as well-rotted manure, bone meal, provide nutrients to the soil in a slow-release form.

Soil Testing: Before fertilizing, it's a good idea to test your soil to determine its nutrient levels.

Application: Fertilizers should be applied at the appropriate times during the papaya plant's growth cycle to support fruit production.

MariGold

NPK Fertilizers: Contain a balanced ratio of nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K), formulations ratio with other micronutrients.

Magnesium (Mg): Magnesium is important for chlorophyll synthesis and enzyme activation in grapevines, can prevent leaf to yellowing and reduced growth.

Calcium (Ca): Calcium-containing fertilizers, such as gypsum or calcium nitrate, can be applied to prevent or correct calcium deficiencies.

Soil Testing: follow recommended application rates and techniques to avoid over-fertilization, which can lead to nutrient imbalances.

Application: Fertilizers should be applied at the appropriate times during the grapevine's growth cycle to support fruit production.
