Know About the Seedling Precautions

We provide all the required measures to take care of different fruits...

Brinjal

Environmental conditions: Sensitive to temperature, humidity, and sunlight may lead to wilting or yellowing leaves.

Germination conditions: If possible, conduct a germination test by planting a few seeds in a separate container to gauge their viability.

Sunlight: Brinjal plants thrive in full sunlight, requiring at least 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight per day.

Air Humidity: In dry climates, consider using mulch or row covers to retain soil moisture and increase humidity around the plants.

Others: Plant seeds at the appropriate depth in the soil sown about ¼ to ½ inch deep.

Cabbage

Environmental conditions: Sensitive to temperature, humidity, and sunlight may lead to wilting or yellowing leaves.

Germination conditions: Pre-planting soaking in water overnight. Scarify seeds to break the coat, aiding moisture penetration.

Sunlight: Cabbage plants thrive in full sunlight, requiring at least 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight per day.

Air Humidity: Adequate humidity can help prevent wilting and reduce the risk of pest and disease problems.

Others: Cabbage plants are cold-hardy but can be damaged and threat by frost to the soil.

Tomato

Environmental conditions: Sensitive to temperature, humidity, and sunlight may lead to wilting or yellowing leaves.

Germination conditions:Scarify or soak the seeds in water overnight before planting to soften the seed coat and promote germination.

Sunlight: Tomato plants require full sunlight to thrive. Choose a planting location that receives at least 6 hours of sunlight.

Air Humidity: In dry climates, consider using mulch or row covers to retain soil moisture and increase humidity.

Others: Tomato plants can be vulnerable to wind damage, especially when young and tender.

Bottle Guard

Environmental conditions: Planting should be done after the last frost date when temperatures are warm.

Germination conditions:soak the seeds in water for 24 hours before planting to soften the seed coat and promote germination.

Sunlight: In hot climates, providing some afternoon shade can help prevent heat stress on the plants.

Air humidity: Bottle gourd plants prefer moderate humidity levels for optimal growth and fruit development.

Others: Bottle gourd plants are sensitive to frost and cold temperatures, plant after threat passed.

Potato

Environmental conditions: Planting in cooler temperatures can help prevent premature sprouting and reduce the risk.

Germination conditions:Requires warm temperatures and consistent moisture, but Old or damaged seeds may have lower germination.

Sunlight: Potatoes require at least 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight per day for optimal growth.

Air humidity: Adequate humidity can help prevent wilting and reduce the risk of foliar diseases.

Others: Strong winds can damage potato plants, especially when they are young and tender.

Leafy vegtables

Environmental conditions: In hot climates, providing some shade during the hottest can help prevent heat stress and bolting.

Germination conditions:check the specific temperature preferences for the type of leafy vegetable you are growing promote germination.

Sunlight: Prefer partial to full sunlight, at least 4 to 6 hours of sunlight.

Avoid overwatering: In dry climates, consider using row covers or misting to increase humidity.

Others: Strong winds can damage tender leafy greens, especially when they are young and fragile.
