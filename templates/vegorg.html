Brinjal

Prepare Soil: Ensure the soil is well-drained with pH between 5.0 and 7.0. Incorporate organic matter to improve soil fertility.

Planting: Plant during the warm months when there's no danger of frost. Dig a hole twice as wide and deep as the root ball of the plant.

Fertilizing: Use compost, aged manure or organic granular fertilizers can be applied to brinjal trees in the spring and summer.

Pest and Disease Management: Use organic pest control methods such as neem oil or insecticidal soap. Encourage natural predators to control pests like aphids and scales.

Prune and Maintain:Prune brinjal trees annually to remove dead or diseased branches and shape the tree. Practice good sanitation to prevent the spread of diseases.

Cabbage

Prepare Soil: These prefer well-drained, fertile soil with a pH between 6.0 and 7.0. Incorporate organic matter to improve soil fertility.

Planting: Plant cabbage trees during the warmer months in a location with full sun exposure.

Fertilizing: Avoid high-nitrogen fertilizers, as they can promote excessive vegetative growth at the expense of vegetable production.

Pest and Disease Management: Use organic pest control methods such as horicultaral oil or insecticidal soap. Encourage natural predators to control pests like mites, honeydew.

Prune and Maintain:Mulch around the base to suppress weeds, and add nutrients to the soil. Practice good sanitation to prevent the spread of diseases.

Tomato

Prepare Soil: These thrives in well-drained, loamy soil with a pH between 6.0 and 8.0. Incorporate organic matter to improve soil fertility.

Planting: Dig a hole twice as wide and deep as the root ball of the plant. Plant in the warm and hot temperature.

Fertilizing: Use the urea and balanced NPK fertilizers followed by the organic fertilizers such as compost and aged manure.

Pest and Disease Management: Use organic pest control methods such as horicultaral oil or spray. Encourage natural predators to control pests like aphids and scales.

Prune and Maintain:Pruning encourages airflow and sunlight penetration, which helps prevent disease and promotes vegetable production.

Bottle Guard

Prepare Soil: Bottle guards prefer well-drained, fertile soil with a pH between 5.5 and 7.0. Incorporate organic matter to improve soil fertility.

Planting: Plant bottle guard suckers or tissue-cultured plants during the warmer months in a location with full sun exposure.

Fertilizing: Use organic granular fertilizers in the spring and summer months, heavy feeders and benefit from regular fertilization.

Pest and Disease Management: Remove and destroy any infected parts to prevent the spread of disease.Encourage natural predators such as ladybugs.

Prune and Maintain:Remove spent flower stems after vegetating to encourage new growth and vegetable production.

Potato

Prepare Soil: Ensure the soil is well-drained with pH between 6.0 and 7.0. Incorporate organic matter to improve soil fertility.

Planting: If starting from seed, sow seeds directly in the planting site or in containers and transplant seedlings once they have several true leaves.

Fertilizing: Apply organic fertilizers such as compost, aged manure, or organic granular fertilizers in the spring and summer months.

Pest and Disease Management: Use organic pest control methods such as neem oil or insecticidal soap. Encourage natural predators to control pests.

Prune and Maintain:Remove any side shoots that develop at the base of the plant to encourage a single-stemmed growth habit.

Leafy Vegetable

Prepare Soil: Ensure the soil is well-drained with pH between 6.0 and 7.0. Avoid excessive nitrogen, which can promote vegetative growth.

Planting: Plant grape vines in the spring or fall, spacing them according to the specific requirements of the variety you're planting.

Fertilizing: USe organic granular fertilizers, avoid excessive fertilization, which can lead to imbalanced growth and poor vegetable quality.

Pest and Disease Management: Monitor leafy vines regularly for signs of pests and diseases. Practice good sanitation by removing and disposing of diseased plant parts promptly.

Prune and Maintain:Apply a layer of organic mulch such as straw, wood chips, around the base of the leafy vines to conserve moisture, suppress weeds, and add nutrients to the soil.
