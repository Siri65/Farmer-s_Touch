Know About the Plant Pests

We provide all the pests that effects the plants & Remidies...

Brinjal

Fruit Fly: This pest lays eggs on young vegetable, Larvae feed inside the brinjal, causing it to rot.

Whitefly: feed on plant sap, causing yellowing, wilting, and excrete honeydew, promoting the growth of sooty mold.

Brinjal Moth: This moth lays eggs on young fruits or leaves. Larvae tunnel into the vegetable, causing damage.

Biological Control: Introduce natural enemies such as ladybugs, lacewings and predatory mites that feed on pests.

Organic Remedies: Neem oil, insecticidal soaps, and horticultural oils helps control pests while being less harmful.

Cabbage

Vegetable Flies: This pest lays eggs on young vegetable, Larvae feed inside the vegetable, causing it to rot.

Mealybugs: These are small, soft-bodied insects that feed on plant sap, causing yellowing of leaves and stunted growth.

Scale Insects: These are small, immobile bumps that feed on vegetable, causing yellowing of leaves and stunted growth.

Biological Control: Introduce natural enemies that feed on pests, biological pesticides containing Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) can be used.

Organic Remedies: Neem oil, insecticidal soaps, removing them with a cotton swab dipped in alcohol.

Tomato

Vegetable Borer: This pest lays eggs on young Vegetable, Larvae feed inside the tomatoes, causing it to rot.

Tomato Leaf Miner: Larvae of this moth create mines in tomato leaves, leading to leaf damage and reduced photosynthesis.

Stem Borer: Larvae of this beetle bore into the stems of tomato plants, causing wilting and dieback.

Biological Control: Apply botanical insecticides or biological control agents like Bacillus thuringiensis.

Organic Remedies: Neem oil, insecticidal soaps, and introducing natural enemies like parasitic wasps.

Bottle Guard

Wild Aphids: Feed on plant sap, causing yellowing, wilting, and excrete honeydew, promoting the growth of sooty mold.

White Weevil: Attack bottle guard plants by tunneling into the corms, pseudostems, and rhizomes, causing damage and rotting.

Bunchy Top Virus: Its a viral disease transmitted by aphids. It causes stunted growth, narrowing of leaves on affected plants.

Biological Control: Introduce natural enemies such as ladybugs, lacewings and predatory mites that feed on pests.

Organic Remedies: Horticultural oil, insecticidal soaps, and introducing natural enemies like parasitic wasps.

Potato

Potato Borers: Borers attack potato, causing damage and rotting. Bugs feed on potato leaves and stems, causing sooty mold growth.

Potato Scale: Scales feed on potato leaves and stems, causing yellowing, wilting, and stunted growth.

Red Spider Mites: Feed on potato leaves, causing stippling, webbing, and leaf discoloration.

Biological Control: Apply sprays on the affected areas, Introduce natural enemies that feed on pests, practicing good sanitation.

Organic Remedies: using traps baited, insecticidal soaps, helps control pests while being less harmful.

Leafy Vegetable

Leafhoppers: Leafhoppers feed on leafy Vegetables, causing stippling, yellowing, and leaf curling.

Phylloxera: Phylloxera feed on Vegetable roots, causing galling, stunting, and reduced vigor.

Berry Moth: Larvae of berry moths feed on leafy Vegetable berries, causing damage and reducing yield.

Biological Control: Pruning and destroying infested buds and shoots, applying insecticides and use pheromone traps.

Organic Remedies: Neem oil, insecticidal soaps, and horticultural oils helps control pests while being less harmful.
