Know About the Fertilizers & Prefertilization Process

We provide all the required methodologies to do before and after Fertilization for different fruits...

Brinjal

Complete Granular Fertilizers: contain a balanced ratio of nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K), formulations like 10-10-10 or 8-8-8 along with other micronutrients.

Slow-Release Fertilizers: It release nutrients gradually over an extended period, providing a steady supply of nutrients to the brinjal plant.

Organic Fertilizers: Organic fertilizers, such as compost, well-rotted manure, bone meal, or blood meal, provide nutrients to the soil in a slow-release form.

Soil Testing: Before fertilizing, it's a good idea to test your soil to determine its pH and nutrient levels.

Application: When applying fertilizer, spread it evenly around the drip line of the tree,Avoid applying fertilizer directly against the trunk, as it can cause root burn.

Micronutrient Fertilizers: These require micronutrients such as magnesium, iron, zinc, manganese, and boron for optimal growth and vegetable production.

Bottle Guard

NPK Fertilizers: Contain a balanced ratio of nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K), formulations ratio, such as 8-10-8 or 10-10-10 with other micronutrients.

Urea: Urea is a nitrogen-rich fertilizer commonly used to promote leaf and stem growth in bottle guard plants for the production of chlorophyll.

Foliar Fertilizers: These are sprayed directly onto the leaves of bottle guard plants, where they are absorbed and translocated throughout the plant.

Soil Testing: Before fertilizing, it's a good idea to test your soil to determine its pH and nutrient levels.

Application: Fertilizers should be applied at the appropriate times during the bottle guard plant's growth cycle to support vegetable production.

Leafy Vegetable
