Chrysanthemums

Prepare Soil: Ensure the soil is well-drained with pH between 6.5 and 7. Incorporate organic matter to improve soil fertility.

Planting: Plant early summer after the last frost has passed. Space the plants according to their mature size, typically 18 to 24 inches apart.

Fertilizing: Apply organic fertilizers such as compost, aged manure, or organic granular fertilizers formulated for flowering plants.

Pest and Disease Management: Use organic pest control methods such as neem oil or insecticidal soap. Encourage natural predators to control pests like aphids and scales.

Prune and Maintain: Pinch back Chrysanthemum plants when they reach about 6 inches tall to encourage bushy growth and more flowers.

Hibiscus

Prepare Soil: These prefer well-drained, fertile soil with a pH between 6.0 and 6.5. Incorporate organic matter to improve soil fertility.

Planting: Plant hibiscus in spring after the last frost has passed. Dig a hole twice as wide as the root ball and at the same depth.

Fertilizing: Apply organic fertilizers such as compost, aged manure, formulated for flowering plants, usually in spring and again in mid-summer.

Pest and Disease Management: Handpick pests when possible, or use organic insecticidal soap or neem oil to control infestations like mites, and whiteflies.

Prune and Maintain:Prune hibiscus to maintain shape and encourage bushy growth. Remove dead or diseased branches, as well as any crossing or overcrowded stems.

Lily

Prepare Soil: These thrives in well-drained, loamy soil with a pH between 6.0 and 7.0. Incorporate organic matter to improve soil fertility.

Planting: Plant lily bulbs in fall or early spring, following the planting depth and spacing recommendations for the specific variety.

Fertilizing: Apply fertilizers according to the package instructions, usually in spring before new growth appears and again after flowering.

Pest and Disease Management: Monitor plants for signs of pests such as slugs, and snails. Handpick pests when possible, or use organic insecticidal soap or neem oil.

Prune and Maintain:Deadhead spent flowers to encourage continuous blooming. Cut the flower stalk back to the base of the plant once all the flowers have faded.

Rose

Prepare Soil: Roses prefer well-drained, fertile soil with a pH between 6.0 and 6.5. Incorporate organic matter to improve soil fertility.

Planting: Plant roses in fall or early spring, following spacing recommendations for the specific variety. Dig a hole deep as the root ball.

Fertilizing: Apply organic fertilizers such as compost, aged manure, or organic granular fertilizers formulated for roses.

Pest and Disease Management: Monitor plants for signs of pests such as Japanese beetles, powdery mildew. Use organic neem oil or horticultural oils.

Prune and Maintain:Prune roses to maintain shape, encourage bushy growth, and remove dead or diseased wood.

Jasmine

Prepare Soil: Ensure the soil is well-drained with pH between 6.0 and 7.0. Incorporate organic matter to improve soil fertility.

Planting: Plant jasmine in spring or early summer after the last frost has passed. Dig a hole twice as wide and deep as the root ball.

Fertilizing: Apply organic fertilizers such as compost, aged manure, or organic granular fertilizers formulated for flowering plants.

Pest and Disease Management: Use organic pest control methods such as neem oil or insecticidal soap. Encourage natural predators to control pests.

Prune and Maintain:Monitor plants for signs of pests such as leaf spot, whiteflies. Use organic insecticidal soap, neem oil.

MariGold

Prepare Soil: Ensure the soil is well-drained with pH between 6.0 and 7.0. Prepare the soil by loosening it to 6-8 inches.

Planting: Sow the seeds 1/4 to 1/2 inch deep and space them about 6-18 inches apart. start them indoors 4-6 weeks before the last frost date.

Fertilizing: Marigolds are not heavy feeders but will benefit from a light application of organic fertilizer, such as compost or a balanced organic fertilizer.

Pest and Disease Management: Marigolds are relatively resistant to pests and diseases, but you may still encounter issues such as aphids, spider mites, or powdery mildew.

Prune and Maintain:Pinch off spent flowers regularly to encourage continuous blooming throughout the growing season. This will also prevent the plants from self-seeding excessively.
