Know About the Plant Pests

We provide all the pests that effects the plants & Remidies...

Chrysanthemums

Aphids: Aphids are soft-bodied insects that suck sap from tissues, causing distorted growth and yellowing of leaves.

Leafhoppers: Leafhoppers are small, wedge-shaped insects that feed on plant sap, causing leaf curling and yellowing.

Powdery Mildew: Powdery mildew is a fungal disease that appears as a white powdery coating on leaves, stems, and flowers.

Biological Control: Introduce natural enemies such as ladybugs, lacewings and predatory mites that feed on pests.

Organic Remedies: Neem oil, insecticidal soaps, and horticultural oils helps control pests while being less harmful.

Hibiscus

Scale insects: Scale insects are small, oval-shaped pests that attach themselves causing yellowing and stunted growth.

Mealybugs: These are small, soft-bodied insects that feed on plant sap, causing yellowing of leaves and stunted growth.

Scale Insects: These are small, immobile bumps that feed on fruits, causing yellowing of leaves and stunted growth.

Biological Control: Introduce natural enemies that feed on pests, biological pesticides containing Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) can be used.

Organic Remedies: Neem oil, insecticidal soaps, removing them with a cotton swab dipped in alcohol.

Lily

Red Lily Beetles: Red lily beetles are bright red with black heads and feed on lily foliage, causing significant damage.

Thrips: Thrips are tiny insects that feed on lily flowers and foliage, causing silvery streaks and distortion.

Lily Leaf Miners: Lily leaf miners are the larvae of certain flies that tunnel through leaves, causing visible trails and damage.

Biological Control: Apply botanical insecticides or biological control agents like Bacillus thuringiensis.

Organic Remedies: Encourage natural predators such as parasitic wasps and use row covers to prevent insects.

Rose

Japanese Beetles: Japanese beetles are metallic green and bronze beetles that skeletonize rose leaves and eat flowers.

Rose Sawflies: Rose sawflies are caterpillar-like larvae that skeletonize rose leaves, leaving behind a lacy pattern.

Rose Midges: Rose midges are tiny flies whose larvae feed on rose buds, causing them to fail to open or develop properly.

Biological Control: Introduce natural enemies such as ladybugs, lacewings and predatory mites that feed on pests.

Organic Remedies: Horticultural oil, insecticidal soaps, and introducing natural enemies like parasitic wasps.

Jasmine

Scale Insects: Scale insects are small, oval-shaped pests that attach themselves causing yellowing and stunted growth.

Leafhoppers: Leafhoppers are small, wedge-shaped insects that feed on plant sap, causing leaf curling and yellowing.

Spider Mites: Spider mites are tiny pests that suck sap from plants, causing yellowing and stippling of leaves.

Biological Control: Apply sprays on the affected areas, Introduce natural enemies that feed on pests, practicing good sanitation.

Organic Remedies: Apply Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) insecticide, a natural bacterial helps control pests.

MariGold

Slugs and Snails: Slugs and snails feed on marigold leaves, causing irregular holes and damage.

Leaf Miners: Leaf miners are larvae of various insects that tunnel through leaves by leaving winding trails.

Damping Off: Damping off is a fungal disease that affects seedlings, causing them to collapse and die.

Biological Control: Pruning and destroying infested buds and shoots, applying insecticides and use pheromone traps.

Organic Remedies:Apply insecticidal soap or horticultural oil helps in control pests while being less harmful.
